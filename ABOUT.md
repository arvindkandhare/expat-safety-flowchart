# About This Project

## What it is

An open-source, community-maintained crisis preparedness framework for Indian nationals abroad. Interactive flowcharts with five threat levels — GREEN through BLACK — covering decision trees, action checklists, and guidance for both the person abroad and their support network in India.

## Why it exists

The Gulf crisis of February-March 2026 put over 8 million Indians in an active conflict zone with limited access to clear, actionable guidance. The Indian government's MADAD portal and embassy helplines exist — but most people don't know about them until they need them. Most of the harm comes not from the crisis itself but from acting on wrong information at the wrong time.

This project started as a personal resource for one person's kith and kin in the Gulf. It became a framework when it became clear the problem was structural: there is no community-maintained, open-source, scenario-specific guide for Indians abroad facing a crisis.

## How it works

Each scenario (Gulf conflict, H1B visa, etc.) is a JSON file. The app loads them dynamically. Contributors add new scenarios or update existing ones without touching application code — a broken Uganda scenario cannot break the Gulf scenario.

Every node in the flowchart can link to a checklist of specific, sourced actions. The five-tier level system (GREEN to BLACK) maps to observable real-world signals — not just official government designations, which tend to lag reality.

## What it is not

- Not official government guidance
- Not legal or medical advice
- Not a news source
- Not a substitute for calling your embassy

## How to contribute

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md). The short version: every addition needs a source. No source, no merge.

New scenarios most needed: Uganda / regime atrocities, Taiwan Strait, student visa (OPT/CPT), Southeast Asia conflict zones.
